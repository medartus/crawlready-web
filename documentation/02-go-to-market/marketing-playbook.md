# CrawlReady Marketing Playbook

**Last Updated:** January 2026
**Document Owner:** Marketing Team
**Review Cycle:** Monthly

---

## Marketing Strategy Overview

### Philosophy
**Product-Led Growth + Content-Led Awareness**

We don't buy customers—we attract them with valuable content and convert them with an exceptional product experience.

### Core Principles
1. **Educate first, sell second** - Build trust through valuable content
2. **Developer credibility** - Speak their language, respect their time
3. **Build in public** - Transparency creates community
4. **Data-driven decisions** - Measure everything, optimize constantly

## Channel Strategy

### Tier 1: Primary Channels (80% of effort)

#### Twitter/X
**Why:** 45.7% of developers active; real-time discussion hub

**Strategy:**
- Daily technical insights
- Build-in-public updates
- Engage with developer community
- Code snippets and tips

**Content Types:**
- Technical threads (AI crawler behavior)
- Product updates
- Industry insights
- Memes (sparingly, with value)

**Metrics:**
- Followers: Target 5K in 6 months
- Engagement rate: 3%+
- Click-through rate: 2%+

#### Hacker News
**Why:** 45% engineers, 23% CTOs - perfect for infrastructure launches

**Strategy:**
- Show HN posts for major launches
- Technical deep-dives
- Participate in relevant discussions
- Build credibility before promotion

**Post Guidelines (from HN admin):**
1. Introduce yourselves (background)
2. Clear 1-sentence description
3. Problem statement
4. Your backstory
5. Solution with technical details
6. Technical differentiation
7. Invite feedback

**Metrics:**
- Front page appearances: 2+ per quarter
- Referral traffic: 5K+ visits per launch
- Account karma: 500+

#### Content Marketing (Blog/SEO)
**Why:** Long-term compounding traffic, thought leadership

**Strategy:**
- Weekly blog posts
- SEO-optimized guides
- Technical deep-dives
- Industry analysis

**Content Calendar Framework:**
| Week | Content Type | Example Topic |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | Problem Awareness | "Why ChatGPT Can't See Your React App" |
| 2 | How-To Guide | "Setting Up CrawlReady in 5 Minutes" |
| 3 | Technical Deep-Dive | "How AI Crawlers Work (Code Examples)" |
| 4 | Industry Analysis | "AI Search Trends: Q1 2026 Report" |

**Metrics:**
- Organic traffic: 10K/mo by Month 6
- Blog→Trial conversion: 5%+
- Domain authority: 30+ by Year 1

### Tier 2: Secondary Channels (15% of effort)

#### GitHub
**Why:** Developers live here; credibility through code

**Strategy:**
- Open-source SDKs (Node.js, Python, Go)
- Free tools (AI Crawler Checker)
- Sponsor relevant repositories
- Contribute to ecosystem

**Tactics:**
- README optimization
- Issue responsiveness
- Community contribution
- Stars/forks tracking

**Metrics:**
- SDK stars: 500+ combined
- SDK downloads: 1K+/mo
- Issue response time: <24hr

#### IndieHackers
**Why:** Startup founders, transparent revenue sharing

**Strategy:**
- Monthly revenue updates
- Build-in-public posts
- Product development stories
- Honest learnings (failures too)

**Metrics:**
- Followers: 500+
- Post engagement: 50+ upvotes avg
- Traffic: 1K+/mo

#### Reddit (r/webdev, r/SaaS, r/SEO)
**Why:** 330M users, deep niche communities

**Strategy:**
- Genuine help first
- Build reputation over time
- Avoid promotional posts
- Answer questions authentically

**Rules:**
- 10:1 value-to-promotion ratio
- Never lead with product
- Be helpful, not salesy
- Build karma organically

**Metrics:**
- Subreddit karma: 1K+ per relevant sub
- Monthly mentions: 10+
- Referral traffic: 500+/mo

### Tier 3: Experimental Channels (5% of effort)

#### LinkedIn
- Thought leadership for VP Marketing persona
- Company page updates
- Industry insights

#### Dev.to / Hashnode
- Cross-post technical content
- Developer community engagement

#### YouTube
- Tutorial videos (future)
- Product demos

#### Podcast Appearances
- Developer podcasts
- SaaS podcasts
- AI/ML podcasts

## Content Strategy

### Three Content Buckets

#### Bucket 1: Discovery Content (60%)
**Purpose:** Attract developers searching for solutions

**Types:**
- SEO-optimized tutorials
- Problem-solving guides
- Technical benchmarks
- Comparison articles

**Examples:**
- "How GPTBot Renders JavaScript (Technical Deep-Dive)"
- "JavaScript Rendering for AI Crawlers: Complete Guide"
- "CrawlReady vs Prerender.io: 2026 Comparison"

#### Bucket 2: Activation Content (25%)
**Purpose:** Convert visitors to users

**Types:**
- Getting started guides
- Integration tutorials
- Use case examples
- Quick wins

**Examples:**
- "5-Minute CrawlReady Setup for React Apps"
- "How to Track AI Citations in Your Dashboard"
- "Integrating CrawlReady with Next.js"

#### Bucket 3: Thought Leadership (15%)
**Purpose:** Establish authority, reach enterprise

**Types:**
- Industry reports
- Trend analysis
- Executive summaries
- Conference talks

**Examples:**
- "State of AI Search 2026 Report"
- "Why AI Search Will Kill Traditional SEO"
- "The Business Case for AI Crawler Optimization"

## Email Marketing

### Sequences

#### Welcome Sequence (5 emails)
1. Day 0: Welcome + Quick Start Guide
2. Day 2: First Win (see your first render)
3. Day 5: Key Features Overview
4. Day 10: Case Study
5. Day 14: Trial Ending + Upgrade CTA

#### Activation Sequence (3 emails)
Triggered when user signs up but doesn't integrate:
1. Day 1: Setup reminder + help offer
2. Day 3: Common issues + documentation
3. Day 7: Final nudge + support chat

#### Expansion Sequence (3 emails)
Triggered at 80% usage:
1. Immediate: Usage alert + plan options
2. Day 3: Feature highlight (higher tier)
3. Day 7: Upgrade offer

## Buyer Psychology Framework

### The Dual-Buyer Reality

Developer tools require convincing two distinct buyers:

1. **Technical Champion** - Evaluates, implements, advocates internally
2. **Business Sponsor** - Approves budget, cares about ROI

**Strategy:** Sell to business problems, enable with developer experience.

### B2B SaaS Buying Reality
- Average buying group: **10-11 stakeholders**
- **70%+** of buyer journey complete before sales contact
- Self-service research dominates early stages
- Technical champion must advocate to business sponsor

## Primary Persona: Technical Founder/CTO "Tom"

### Demographics
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Age | 28-38 |
| Role | CTO, Technical Co-founder, Lead Engineer |
| Company | SaaS startup, $500K-$5M ARR |
| Team Size | 5-30 engineers |
| Tech Stack | React/Next.js, Vue/Nuxt, or Angular SPA |

### Psychographics
- **Values:** Speed, efficiency, developer experience, autonomy
- **Fears:** Missing market opportunities, technical debt, wasting time
- **Goals:** Product-market fit, growth, competitive advantage
- **Decision Style:** Data-driven, tries before buying, trusts peers

### Jobs to Be Done

**Functional Jobs:**
1. Get our JavaScript site visible in ChatGPT answers
2. Understand which AI platforms are indexing our content
3. Track when competitors get cited vs us
4. Implement without rebuilding our entire architecture

**Emotional Jobs:**
1. Feel confident we're not missing the AI search wave
2. Reduce anxiety about invisible competitive threats
3. Demonstrate technical leadership to team/investors

**Social Jobs:**
1. Be seen as forward-thinking by peers
2. Share insights with other founders
3. Stay ahead of less technical competitors

### Pain Points (Detailed)

**Pain 1: "My React app doesn't show up in ChatGPT answers"**
- Severity: High
- Frequency: Daily (sees competitors cited)
- Current Solution: None (or ignoring it)
- Emotional: Frustration, FOMO, anxiety

**Pain 2: "Competitors with WordPress rank higher in AI search"**
- Severity: High
- Frequency: Weekly
- Current Solution: Considering SSR rebuild ($120K+)
- Emotional: Unfairness, competitive pressure

**Pain 3: "No visibility into whether AI crawlers can access my content"**
- Severity: Medium
- Frequency: Ongoing uncertainty
- Current Solution: Manual testing (time-consuming)
- Emotional: Uncertainty, lack of control

**Pain 4: "Engineering team focused on product, not infrastructure"**
- Severity: High
- Frequency: Constant tradeoff
- Current Solution: Deprioritize AI SEO
- Emotional: Resource constraints, guilt

### Decision Criteria
1. **Setup Speed:** Must be <15 minutes (ideally <5)
2. **No Maintenance:** Zero ongoing engineering work
3. **Transparent Pricing:** Know exactly what we'll pay
4. **API-First:** Programmatic control available
5. **Proof Points:** Other startups using it successfully

### Common Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We'll just rebuild with SSR" | SSR takes 6 months and $120K+. We take 5 minutes. |
| "AI search isn't important yet" | ChatGPT has 800M users. Your competitors are already there. |
| "Prerender.io is cheaper/established" | We're 46% cheaper and AI-first with citation tracking they don't have. |
| "We can build this ourselves" | Sure, at 20 hours/month maintenance. Or use us for $49/mo. |

## Buyer Journey Map

### Stage 1: Problem Awareness
**Trigger Events:**
- Notice competitor cited in ChatGPT, not them
- Organic traffic declining
- CEO/board asks about AI search
- See article about AI search trends

**Information Needs:**
- Is this a real problem?
- How big is the impact?
- Are competitors affected too?

**CrawlReady Touchpoints:**
- Blog: "Why Your JavaScript Site is Invisible to ChatGPT"
- Free Tool: AI Crawler Checker
- Social: Problem awareness content

### Stage 2: Solution Research
**Actions:**
- Search for solutions
- Ask peers/network
- Compare options
- Read reviews/case studies

**Information Needs:**
- What solutions exist?
- How do they compare?
- What do others use?

**CrawlReady Touchpoints:**
- Comparison pages vs Prerender.io
- Documentation (evaluate technical approach)
- Case studies with real results

### Stage 3: Evaluation
**Actions:**
- Sign up for trial
- Test with own site
- Share with team/engineering
- Calculate ROI

**Information Needs:**
- Does it work for my site?
- How hard is implementation?
- What's the total cost?

**CrawlReady Touchpoints:**
- Free trial (full features)
- Documentation (setup guides)
- Support (answer questions)
- ROI calculator

### Stage 4: Decision
**Actions:**
- Get internal approval
- Compare final options
- Negotiate (enterprise)
- Purchase

**Information Needs:**
- Final pricing
- Contract terms
- Support/SLA
- Security/compliance

**CrawlReady Touchpoints:**
- Pricing page (transparent)
- Security documentation
- Customer support
- Onboarding call (if requested)

### Stage 5: Implementation
**Actions:**
- Technical setup
- Configure settings
- Test and validate
- Go live

**Information Needs:**
- Setup instructions
- Best practices
- Troubleshooting

**CrawlReady Touchpoints:**
- Setup documentation
- Technical support
- Onboarding emails
- Success metrics dashboard

### Stage 6: Value Realization
**Actions:**
- Monitor results
- See first citations
- Share wins internally
- Consider expansion

**Information Needs:**
- Is it working?
- How to optimize?
- What else can we do?

**CrawlReady Touchpoints:**
- Analytics dashboard
- Performance reports
- Success team check-ins
- Feature announcements

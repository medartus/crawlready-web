# Product

## Register

brand

Default register is brand (landing page, public diagnostic, marketing surfaces). Override to product for dashboard, onboarding, and authenticated app surfaces.

## Users

Technical founders and dev leads building with JavaScript frameworks (React, Next.js, Vue, Angular). They arrive from search, Hacker News, or technical blog posts. They suspect their site is partially or fully invisible to AI crawlers but lack a clear way to diagnose the problem or quantify the gap.

Their context: mid-workflow, evaluating whether AI visibility is a real issue for their site. They want a fast answer, not a sales pitch. They respect tools that show them data and let them decide.

## Product Purpose

CrawlReady produces a free AI Readiness Score (0-100) with a visual diff showing exactly what AI crawlers see versus what humans see. The diagnostic is the core value proposition. The paid tier intercepts AI crawler requests and serves clean, structured content while human visitors see the normal site.

Phase 0 delivers: landing page, working diagnostic tool, and analytics onboarding at crawlready.app.

Success looks like: a technical visitor enters a URL, immediately sees a meaningful diagnostic, understands the problem, and trusts the tool enough to create an account.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, builder-friendly.

The voice is factual and direct. No marketing hyperbole, no urgency theater. CrawlReady speaks like a senior engineer explaining a tricky problem to a peer: clear, specific, and respectful of the reader's intelligence. Confidence comes from showing real data, not from bold claims.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS templates**: cookie-cutter hero + three feature cards + pricing grid. No identity, interchangeable with any other tool. CrawlReady should feel like it was built by someone who cares, not assembled from a template.
- **SEO tool dashboards (Ahrefs, SEMrush)**: dense, data-heavy, cluttered navigation. Walls of metrics that overwhelm instead of clarify. CrawlReady shows less, but shows it better.
- **Neon-on-dark developer hype**: glow effects, particle backgrounds, terminal aesthetics. Feels like a crypto project or a hackathon demo. CrawlReady is a serious tool, not a tech demo.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** The visual diff diagnostic IS the product. Let the tool demonstrate value instead of claiming it. Every landing page section should prove something, not just assert it.
2. **Data with clarity.** Present technical data (scores, diffs, recommendations) so it's immediately actionable, not overwhelming. One clear insight beats ten raw metrics.
3. **Earned trust.** Build credibility through precision and transparency, not through marketing superlatives. If the data speaks, the copy can stay quiet.
4. **Builder respect.** Treat the audience as competent. No dumbing down, no hand-holding, no unnecessary explanations. Technical users notice when you respect their time.
5. **Quiet confidence.** The design should feel like it doesn't need to try hard. Quality speaks for itself. Restraint is a feature.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Sufficient color contrast ratios (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text), full keyboard navigation, screen reader support, focus indicators, and semantic HTML throughout. Reduced-motion preferences respected for all animations.
